# 📝 Bio Review Rubric

This document defines the evaluation framework used by Mentosync for reviewing applicant biographies.

The purpose of this rubric is to capture my application review philosophy in a structured way so that Mentosync can gradually learn how I evaluate applicant bios.

---

# Evaluation Criteria

## 1. Story & Context

### What am I evaluating?

Whether the applicant introduces themselves through a meaningful background story or personal experience.

### Why does it matter?

A strong background story provides context, helps the reader understand the applicant, and establishes the foundation for the rest of the bio.

### Review Questions

- Does the bio have a background story connected to it?
- Does the applicant clearly explain the story?
- Does the story naturally introduce the applicant?

### Strong Response

- Personal and meaningful
- Easy to understand
- Creates context for the rest of the bio

---

## 2. Problem Awareness

### What am I evaluating?

Whether the applicant identifies a meaningful problem or challenge.

### Why does it matter?

Applicants who recognize important problems often demonstrate awareness, curiosity, and purpose.

### Review Questions

- Does the applicant identify a problem?
- Is the problem clearly explained?
- Does the applicant explain why the problem matters?
- Are statistics or evidence used appropriately to demonstrate the scale of the problem?

### Strong Response

The reader clearly understands:

- What the problem is
- Why it matters
- Who it affects

---

## 3. Personal Connection

### What am I evaluating?

Whether the identified problem is personally connected to the applicant.

### Why does it matter?

Personal experiences often lead to more authentic motivation and stronger purpose.

### Review Questions

- Is the problem personally related to the applicant?
- Is the problem connected to the applicant's background story?
- Does the connection feel genuine?

### Strong Response

The applicant's experiences naturally explain why they care about solving the problem.

---

## 4. Initiative & Action

### What am I evaluating?

Whether the applicant has taken initiative toward solving the problem.

### Why does it matter?

Recognizing a problem is important, but taking action demonstrates leadership and commitment.

### Review Questions

- Does the applicant show initiative?
- Has the applicant attempted to solve the problem?
- Does the bio demonstrate leadership or action?

### Strong Response

The applicant provides clear evidence of action, leadership, research, volunteering, projects, or innovation.

---

## 5. Authenticity

### What am I evaluating?

Whether the bio feels genuine, sincere, and believable.

### Why does it matter?

Authenticity creates trust and allows the applicant's personality to come through.

### Review Questions

- Does the bio read naturally?
- Does the writing feel genuine?
- Does the applicant avoid exaggeration?

### Strong Response

The applicant sounds honest, reflective, and authentic.

---

## 6. Interests & Motivation

### What am I evaluating?

Whether the applicant clearly explains their interests and why those interests developed.

### Why does it matter?

A clear explanation of motivation demonstrates maturity and intentionality.

### Review Questions

- Does the applicant explain their interests?
- Does the applicant explain why those interests developed?
- Is the motivation convincing?

### Strong Response

The applicant's interests are clearly connected to their experiences and future aspirations.

---

## 7. Purpose & Direction

### What am I evaluating?

Whether the bio communicates a clear purpose and direction.

### Why does it matter?

A strong bio should leave the reader understanding what drives the applicant.

### Review Questions

- Does the bio have a clear purpose?
- Does the applicant communicate future direction?
- Does the bio leave a lasting impression?

### Strong Response

The bio clearly communicates who the applicant is, what motivates them, and where they hope to make an impact.

---

## 8. Writing Quality

### What am I evaluating?

The effectiveness of the applicant's writing.

### Why does it matter?

Strong ideas lose impact if they are difficult to understand.

### Review Questions

- Is the writing clear?
- Does the bio flow logically?
- Are transitions smooth?
- Is the writing concise?
- Is the bio engaging?

### Strong Response

The writing is clear, organized, concise, and enjoyable to read.

---

# Positive Signals

These characteristics immediately strengthen a bio.

- Strong personal narrative
- Clear purpose
- Genuine voice
- Personal connection to the problem
- Initiative and leadership
- Reflection and growth
- Clear motivation
- Specific examples
- Logical organization

---

# Red Flags

These characteristics weaken a bio.

- Generic storytelling
- No personal connection
- No clear purpose
- Claims without evidence
- Lack of initiative
- Excessive exaggeration
- Weak organization
- Repetition
- Vague motivation
- Overuse of clichés

---

# Scoring Guide

| Score | Interpretation |
|--------|----------------|
| **9–10** | Outstanding — exceeds expectations with little or no weakness. |
| **7–8** | Strong — demonstrates most qualities with only minor weaknesses. |
| **5–6** | Average — meets expectations but has noticeable gaps. |
| **3–4** | Weak — several important elements are missing. |
| **1–2** | Very Weak — fails to demonstrate the criterion effectively. |
